Abstract—OpenHarmony emerges as a potential third pillar in the mobile OS landscape alongside Android and iOS. To promote the adoption of OpenHarmony, developers have been porting C/C++ libraries from the native OS (Linux) to Open- Harmony. However, the porting can be exceptionally challenging because of the discrepancies between Linux and OpenHarmony in system libraries, runtime environments and build systems. These discrepancies lead to Cross-platform Compatibility (CPC) issues during cross compilation, which can be difficult to address for two reasons. First, existing cross-compilation toolchains only return simple error messages upon the occurrence of CPC issues, which are often not adaquate for the diagnosis of CPC issues. Second, fixing CPC issues requires the understanding and identification of discrepancies between the two OSs, yet documentation or systematic guidelines about the discrepancies are often not available.

To assist developers in addressing these challenges, we con- ducted an empirical study on 92 C/C++ libraries successfully ported to OpenHarmony. Through manual step-by-step repro- duction of all CPC issues, our study reveals that discrepancies between OpenHarmony and Linux can be divided into three cat- egories, and CPC issues can manifest through eight dimensions. Furthermore, we identified eight common adaptation strategies for addressing CPC issues. Based on these findings, we developed a novel technique CROSS2OH, which seamlessly automates the porting of C/C++ libraries from Linux to OpenHarmony. Our evaluation shows that CROSS2OH achieves 0.81 and 0.89 in addressing CPC issues. Besides, with the aid of CROSS2OH, 26 libraries have been officially ported to OpenHarmony.
